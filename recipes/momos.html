Chicken Momos

Description

Momo are bite-size dumplings made with a spoonful of stuffing wrapped in dough with origins from Tibet. Momo are usually steamed, though they are sometimes fried or steam-fried. Meat or vegetables fillings becomes succulent as it produces an intensively flavored broth sealed inside the wrappers

Ingredients

  • 1 + 1/2 cup All-Purpose Flour
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • 1 cup Chicken (minced)
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • 1 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper
  • 1 tsp Green Pepper (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 1/4 cup Spring Onions (chopped)
  • 1 + 1/2 tbsp Hot Cooking Oil

Steps To Prepare

  1. Add flour and salt to a large bowl then gradually add water to the flour. Whisk the flour well with the water and continue adding water until it forms a smooth dough. Knead this dough well, then cover it with a damp cloth then leave it for 1 hour.
  2. Take a suitable wok and add minced chicken, then stir in salt, soy sauce, black pepper, green onions, green chili, ginger-garlic paste, and hot oil. Mix the meat well using a spatula and keep it aside.
  3. Knead the set dough again and divide it into two equal parts. Roll each part into 2 inches-thick ropes. Slice these ropes into 3 inches-thick pieces.
  4. Roll each dough piece into a 1/6-inch-thick round and add-a tablespoon of chicken filling, then wet the edges of the dough. Seal the edges together around the filling and pinch them to make a dumpling.
  5. Use the entire filling to make as many dumplings as you can.
  6. Now grease a medium sides steamer basket and grease it with cooking spray. Place 5-6 dumplings into the greased steamer. Boil 2 cups of water in a cooking pot and place the steamer basket over the boiling water.
  7. Cover the dumplings with a lid and steam the dumplings for 12 minutes. Once done, transfer the dumplings to the serving plate. Cooking the remaining dumplings in a similar way.
  8. Serve warm to get the best taste from this delicious recipe.
